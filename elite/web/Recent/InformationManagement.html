Information Management

Many companies today invest millions to establish an Information Management solution, but often leave the real value of these solutions untapped. Turning all that data and information into a strategic corporate asset requires a Business Intelligence solution – a flexible mix of analytic, reporting and business process competencies. And that requires moving beyond a mindset of standalone project development and maintenance.

Realizing competitive advantage from your Information Management solution takes technical ability, sensitivity to business needs and management experience in equal measure. Elite Software Solutions Business Intelligence Competency Center (BICC) offers a time-tested, cost-effective way for a company to break the development paradox and advance the maturity of these critical systems. Our BICC professionals deliver application support, solution management, business process support, data governance and quality support related services.  

Misconceptions about maintenance
Two common misconceptions spell trouble for many Information Management solutions. One is that creating the system is an end in itself – once the ribbon is cut, new needs will not emerge. The other is that Business Intelligence solutions are self-sustaining – the new infrastructure and applications can be maintained for minimal cost.

Going live with an initial set of metrics, reports and dashboards is only the beginning. To have the right impact, a Business Intelligence solution needs a sustainable, cost-effective combination of technical, functional, industry-specific capabilities and know-how to evolve along with the needs of the organization it was built to serve. On the surface, these aims seem easy to identify and manage. In practice, the demand for change and the need to support each new project, if not managed under an enterprise Information Management program, can lead to standalone projects and ever-increasing maintenance workloads. This can eventually reach a breaking point, where a majority of the resources become devoted to simply keeping the system operating. Evolution and enhancement are choked off. The company becomes the servant of its Information Management solution instead of its master. But when a company breaks out of this development paradox, the Business Intelligence solution can help them achieve better quality data, more timely reporting and business process support that makes a strategic difference.

Four ways to get more value now

Don't stop at go-live. Benefit realization starts when the business intelligence or data warehouse solution goes live. But just as a race is never won as soon as the starting gun goes off, the full value of your investment can't be realized immediately after deployment. A company's return on its business intelligence investment will only be realized when the business starts making decisions enabled by the new information management solution.

View business intelligence as strategic, not transactional. Information Management solutions support strategic, mission critical, time-sensitive analysis. A "keep the lights on" approach won't deliver responsive support grounded in functional and technical knowledge. Know the application is not the end; it's the beginning.

Insist on solution evolution. Information Management solutions are typically a mix of standalone applications and tools. Optimizing that mix over time requires a regular infusion of innovation focused on business-technology integration, architecture and application functionality.

Coordinate. Information Management solutions are often tightly integrated with ERP applications. Changes in these systems can have a direct impact on the business intelligence solution and hence the company's analysis and reporting ability. Effective business intelligence maintenance requires close coordination with all the boundary systems.
